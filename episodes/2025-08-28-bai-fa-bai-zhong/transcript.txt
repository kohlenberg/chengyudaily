Welcome to Chengyu Bites — your quick summary on Chinese 成语. Ever wondered how precision is captured in a single phrase? [break 1s] The phrase is: 百发百中, bǎi fā bǎi zhòng. [break 1s] 百 (bǎi) means 'hundred' [break 0.5s] 发 (fā) means 'to shoot' [break 0.5s] 百 (bǎi) again means 'hundred' [break 0.5s] 中 (zhòng) means 'to hit' or 'to be successful.' [break 1s] So, 百发百中 literally translates to 'hundred shots, hundred hits,' which figuratively means hitting the target every time or being highly accurate. [break 1s] Here’s the story behind it: [break 1.5s] This idiom originates from archery, where a skilled archer could shoot a hundred arrows and hit the target every single time. It reflects not only skill but also confidence in one’s abilities. Over time, it has expanded beyond archery to describe anyone who consistently achieves success. [break 1.5s] Example 1: 他在比赛中百发百中。 [break 1s] He hit the target every time in the competition. [break 1s] Example 2: 她的演讲真是百发百中。 [break 1s] Her speech was spot on every time. [break 1s] Example 3: 这位医生的诊断总是百发百中。 [break 1s] This doctor’s diagnoses are always accurate. [break 1s] So remember, 百发百中 means to hit the target every time! Thank you for listening!
