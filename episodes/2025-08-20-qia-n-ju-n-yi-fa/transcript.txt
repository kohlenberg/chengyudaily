Welcome to Chengyu Bites — your quick summary on Chinese 成语. Today, we explore a phrase that describes being in a precarious situation. [break 1s] The phrase is: 千钧一发, qiān jūn yī fà. [break 0.5s] 千 (qiān) means 'thousand' [break 0.5s] 钧 (jūn) means 'pound' [break 0.5s] 一 (yī) means 'one' [break 0.5s] 发 (fà) means 'hair' [break 0.5s] Full idiom again: 千钧一发 (A thousand pounds hanging by a hair); it figuratively refers to being in a critical or dangerous situation. [break 1s] Here’s the story behind it: [break 1.5s] This idiom originates from ancient China, where a 'jūn' is a unit of weight. Imagine a heavy weight, like a thousand pounds, delicately balanced on a single hair. If that hair snaps, the weight falls, signifying the precariousness of the situation. Over time, this expression evolved into a metaphor for moments where everything hangs in the balance. [break 1.5s] Example one: 这次考试对她来说真是千钧一发。 [break 1s] This exam is truly a critical moment for her. [break 1s] Example two: 他们的决定像千钧一发，必须小心。 [break 1s] Their decision is like a thousand pounds hanging by a hair; they must be careful. [break 1s] Example three: 在比赛的最后几秒，比分平局，情况千钧一发。 [break 1s] In the last few seconds of the game, with the score tied, the situation is critical. [break 1s] So, that’s 千钧一发, which means a critical moment. Thank you for listening to Chengyu Bites! See you next time for another idiom. [break 1s]
