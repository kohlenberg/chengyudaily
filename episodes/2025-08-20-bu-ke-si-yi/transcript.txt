Welcome to Chengyu Bites — your quick summary on Chinese 成语. Today, we explore an idiom that captures the essence of the unbelievable. [break 1s] The phrase is: 不可思议, bù kě sī yì. [break 1s] 不 (bù) - not [break 0.5s] 可 (kě) - possible [break 0.5s] 思 (sī) - to think [break 0.5s] 议 (yì) - to discuss [break 1s] The full idiom again: 不可思议 literally means 'not possible to think' and figuratively means 'inconceivable; beyond imagination.' [break 1s] Here’s the story behind it: [break 1.5s] This idiom originated from ancient Chinese literature, where it described events or situations that were so extraordinary that they defied logic and understanding. People would use it to express their astonishment at miraculous happenings or feats that seemed impossible. Over time, it became a popular way to describe anything that left one speechless in disbelief. [break 1.5s] Example one: 他的成功真是不可思议。 [break 1s] His success is truly inconceivable. [break 1s] Example two: 这部电影的情节不可思议。 [break 1s] The plot of this movie is unbelievable. [break 1s] Example three: 他们的友谊经过了不可思议的考验。 [break 1s] Their friendship has withstood inconceivable challenges.
