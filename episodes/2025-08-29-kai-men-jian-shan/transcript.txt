Welcome to Chengyu Bites — your quick summary on Chinese 成语. Ever wondered how to cut through the fluff in a conversation? [break 1s] The phrase is: 开门见山, kāi mén jiàn shān. [break 0.5s] 开 (kāi) means 'to open' [break 0.5s] 门 (mén) means 'door' [break 0.5s] 见 (jiàn) means 'to see' [break 0.5s] 山 (shān) means 'mountain' [break 0.5s] Full idiom again: 开门见山 literally means 'open the door and see the mountain.' Figuratively, it signifies getting straight to the point. [break 1s] Here’s the story behind it: [break 1.5s] This idiom originates from a time when travelers would arrive at a mountain pass. Upon opening the door to a valley, the majestic mountain would be immediately visible. This imagery suggests that one should get to the essence of a matter without unnecessary preamble. [break 1.5s] Examples: [break 1s] 1. 会议开始时，他开门见山地提出了问题。 [break 1s] During the meeting, he got straight to the point with the issue. [break 1s] 2. 在讨论中，我们应该开门见山，避免浪费时间。 [break 1s] In discussions, we should be direct to avoid wasting time. [break 1s] 3. 她总是开门见山，让人觉得很舒服。 [break 1s] She always gets to the point, making people feel at ease. [break 1s] So, the idiom again: 开门见山 means to get straight to the point.
