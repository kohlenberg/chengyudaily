Welcome to Chengyu Bites — your quick summary on Chinese 成语. Today, we explore the idea of putting in hard work for long-term benefits. [break 1s] The phrase is: 一劳永逸, yī láo yǒng yì. [break 1s] 一 (yī) - one [break 0.5s] 劳 (láo) - labor/work [break 0.5s] 永 (yǒng) - forever/eternal [break 0.5s] 奕 (yì) - ease/comfort [break 0.5s] Full idiom again: 一劳永逸 means 'One effort for lasting ease.' [break 1s] Here’s the story behind it: [break 1.5s] This idiom originates from the idea that if you put in the necessary effort and hard work now, you can avoid future troubles and enjoy lasting comfort. It emphasizes the value of diligence and foresight in achieving lasting results. In ancient times, farmers who worked hard during the planting season would ensure a good harvest, allowing them to relax later. [break 1.5s] Example 1: 你现在努力学习，以后就可以一劳永逸。 [break 1s] If you study hard now, you can enjoy lasting ease later. [break 1s] Example 2: 他为了这个项目加班，想要一劳永逸地解决问题。 [break 1s] He worked overtime for this project to solve the problem once and for all. [break 1s] Example 3: 投资教育是一劳永逸的选择。 [break 1s] Investing in education is a choice for lasting benefits. [break 1s] So, that was 一劳永逸, meaning 'One effort for lasting ease.'
