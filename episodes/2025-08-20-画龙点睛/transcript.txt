Welcome to Chengyu Bites — your quick summary on Chinese 成语. Today, we explore the art of giving life to your creations. [break 1s] The phrase is: 画龙点睛, huà lóng diǎn jīng. [break 1s] 画 (huà) - to draw [break 0.5s] 龙 (lóng) - dragon [break 0.5s] 点 (diǎn) - to dot or to touch [break 0.5s] 睛 (jīng) - eye [break 0.5s] Full idiom again: 画龙点睛 literally means 'to draw a dragon and dot its eyes,' figuratively meaning to add the finishing touch; to bring something to life. [break 1s] Here’s the story behind it: [break 1.5s] In ancient times, there was a famous painter named Zhang Sengyao who was exceptional at painting dragons. He once painted a dragon, but it lacked life until he added the pupils to its eyes. Once completed, the dragon appeared so real that it seemed ready to fly away! This is how the phrase came to symbolize the crucial final touch that brings something to life. [break 1.5s] Example 1: 他的演讲真的很精彩，最后一句话让人觉得画龙点睛。 [break 1s] His speech was incredible; the last sentence really added the finishing touch. [break 1s] Example 2: 这幅画很美，但我觉得还缺少一点什么，可能需要画龙点睛。 [break 1s] This painting is beautiful, but I feel it lacks something. It might need that final touch. [break 1s] Example 3: 在写作中，好的结尾总是能起到画龙点睛的效果。 [break 1s] In writing, a good ending always serves as the finishing touch. [break 1s] So, today we learned about 画龙点睛, which means to add the finishing touch. Thanks for listening to Chengyu Bites! See you next time for another idiom. [break 1s]
