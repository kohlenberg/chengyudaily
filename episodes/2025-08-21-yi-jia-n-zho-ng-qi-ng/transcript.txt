Welcome to Chengyu Bites — your quick summary on Chinese 成语. Today’s idiom is all about that instant spark of attraction. [break 1s] The phrase is: 一见钟情, yī jiàn zhōng qíng. [break 1s] 一 (yī) - one [break 0.5s] 见 (jiàn) - see [break 0.5s] 钟 (zhōng) - bell or to hit [break 0.5s] 情 (qíng) - feelings or love [break 0.5s] Full idiom again: 一见钟情 means literally 'see once and hit love,' which figuratively describes falling in love at first sight. [break 1s] Here’s the story behind it: [break 1.5s] This idiom originates from a famous love story in ancient China, where a young scholar saw a beautiful woman at first sight and was instantly captivated by her charm. This moment of profound attraction led to a passionate romance, illustrating that sometimes love can strike like a bell ringing unexpectedly. [break 1.5s] Example 1: 他对她一见钟情。 [break 1s] He fell in love with her at first sight. [break 1s] Example 2: 他们的爱情故事是典型的一见钟情。 [break 1s] Their love story is a classic case of love at first sight. [break 1s] Example 3: 许多人相信一见钟情是真实的。 [break 1s] Many people believe that love at first sight is real. [break 1s] So, remember: 一见钟情 means love at first sight. Thanks for listening to Chengyu Bites!
